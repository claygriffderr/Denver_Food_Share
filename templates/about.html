The Goal: Neighbors Feeding Neighbors

Between the reduction of federal SNAP benefits and the rising costs of inflation, too many people in local communities are being forced to skip meals. It is a shared belief that no one should go hungry while perfectly good food is thrown away.

What Sharing Looks Like

This application is a hyper-local, community-driven social forum designed to bridge the gap between food waste and food insecurity. It is a dedicated space for neighbors to post leftover food, offer groceries, and look out for one another. Contributors do not need to be professional chefs to participate. Sharing comes in many forms, and every contribution matters:

  • The Corporate Surplus: Bringing home the untouched, catered sandwich platters left over after a corporate board meeting.
  • The Garden Bounty: Offering up the extra tomatoes, zucchini, or herbs from a summer garden that cannot be eaten fast enough.
  • The Big Batch: Purposely making an extra-large batch of a weekly chili or lasagna specifically to portion out and share with someone nearby.
  • The Pantry Cleanse: Giving away unopened, shelf-stable groceries that were bought but never used.

Community Guidelines

To keep this platform safe, welcoming, and strictly focused on community care, all users must agree to the following guidelines.

Category Rule Expectation
Food Safety Honesty is Mandatory Always accurately label food using the dietary tags and preparation states.
Food Safety No Compromised Food There is a strict reliance on community self-policing. The sharing of previously served food (e.g., food scraped off a plate) or items that are clearly spoiled is absolutely forbidden.
Food Safety Proper Handling Ensure food is stored safely before pickup, utilizing the "Refrigerated" or "Frozen" tags to communicate how quickly a meal needs to be transported.
Respect & Decorum Zero Financial Exchange This is a place for giving, not selling. Because the platform has no exchange of payment, it remains a true social exchange. Never ask for money, and never offer money.
Respect & Decorum Protect Personal Info Do not post phone numbers, emails, or exact home addresses in public listings. Always use the secure, internal messaging system to coordinate pickup details.
Respect & Decorum Be a Good Neighbor Show up when agreed upon, respect people's privacy when picking up food, and treat everyone with dignity.

Additional Project-Specific Information

Because the app is built with a specific technical stack (encrypted emails, radius filtering instead of exact addresses), the following privacy and liability protections are hard-coded into the experience:

  • Privacy First: How Users Are Protected: Exact home addresses are never exposed; only zip codes are used to calculate a general distance radius for the neighborhood map. Furthermore, email addresses are mathematically encrypted in the database, and the in-app messaging system ensures meals can be claimed without ever exposing personal contact information to strangers.
  • Liability Disclaimer: The "At Your Own Risk" Clause: As a digital facilitator of a social exchange, this platform does not inspect, handle, or guarantee the safety of the food posted. By using this app, individuals acknowledge that they are accepting food entirely at their own risk.
  • Good Samaritan Laws: The platform operates in the spirit of the Bill Emerson Good Samaritan Food Donation Act, which protects individuals who donate food in good faith from civil and criminal liability.
